where we align, where we don't
TermRecommended definitionAlignmentWhy this / where confusion lives
OFFERINGAnother way to say "services" without saying "PS service". Identifies a service category encompassing multiple delivery approaches. Not directly sellable by itself.47%Industry standard (Salesforce, ServiceNow) treats "offering" as a portfolio category, not a transaction. Keeping it non-sellable protects the hierarchy: Offering → Package / SOW / RSA.
PACKAGEA pre-configured, fixed-scope PS service sold as a SKU. Purchased without a SOW — only the datasheet needs to be shared. Delivered end-to-end with predictable timeline and deliverables.67%27% "flexible bundle" camp is the risk. The fix isn't changing the definition — it's tightening ICP and pre-requisite checks. "Fixed-scope" is the non-negotiable anchor.
RSA (prev. TSM)A limited-term, embedded PS role combining strategic guidance and hands-on delivery. Focused on observability architecture, platform standards, and long-term adoption. Has its own SKU and datasheet. Does not include an EM by default.40%Highest ambiguity of any offering term. Revisit what changed when TSM → RSA. "Ongoing advisory only" undersells it; "staff augmentation" undersells the strategic component.
IMPLEMENTATIONA PS-led engagement to design, configure, and build an agreed NEW BUILD Grafana solution. Includes architecture, hands-on deployment, knowledge transfer, and handoff to the customer team.80%Strongest consensus. Minor clean-up: remove the implicit "new to Grafana" framing (13% held). Implementation applies to any new build, not just first-time customers.
MIGRATIONA PS-led engagement to move a customer's existing monitoring or competitor observability stack to Grafana. Covers moves from competitors, OSS, and in-house solutions. Always qualify the sub-type in the SOW (e.g. "Competitor Migration — Datadog to Grafana Cloud").53% + 40% "all of above"The definition is fine — the fix is operational. Requiring a migration sub-type qualifier in every SOW is a one-line template change that eliminates most ambiguity.
ADVISORYAn expert consulting engagement providing strategic and technical guidance without hands-on delivery. Includes architecture reviews, best-practice recommendations, design workshops, and roadmap planning. Customer retains implementation responsibility. Advisory ≠ hands-on hours.80%Strong consensus. Key phrase to reinforce: "customer retains implementation responsibility." RSA may include hands-on work — Advisory does not.
DEPLOYMENTInstalling, configuring, and enabling a Grafana environment to go live in a target infrastructure. A technical phase or activity within an Implementation or Migration — not a standalone offering. Can refer to any environment promotion, not just production go-live.53%33% included "adoption and training" — conflating Deployment with Onboarding. Deployment is a technical event (go-live), not an enablement activity.
ONBOARDING⚠ Recommend we do NOT use "Onboarding" as a standard PS term. 13% said it only applies to new hires (a Grafana company term), and the remaining split three ways. We already have terms for all the activities it could describe.47% — 3-way splitHighest ambiguity of any non-offering term. Three models at near-equal frequency. Retiring the term avoids confusion with Sales and OAs who need to be clear on PS scope.
OUTCOMEA measurable business or technical result achieved by the customer as a direct result of the PS engagement. Distinct from deliverables (what PS produces) and milestones (when things happen).73%Good consensus. 20% use Outcome and Milestone interchangeably — the distinction matters for SOW success criteria: deliverables are completed, milestones are reached, outcomes are measured.

and evolve offerings

Recommendation
Build from PS delivery reality first, then translate into customer value language for the sale. PS delivery constraints are fixed. Customer value is how we describe it. Sales language is the wrapper.

Test: if an EM/SA can't explain what they'll do in the first month, the offering isn't ready to sell.
ONE thing every offering must have
Clarity in scoping: what's sold, why, to whom, and the value it provides — strong scoping and clear expectations
Customer outcomes: specific, billable outcome with a clear value proposition aligned to customer needs
Operational support: consistent resources, processes for CX alignment, technical requirements gathering, and engagement management
Adaptation: continuously evolve with iteration; plan to mitigate risks for different customer segments and product roadmap changes
Quick wins
Rigorous yet nimbler scoping: deeper discovery, clear scope of work
Improved pre-sales materials: decision trees, real value definitions (no fluff), clear route to activation
Standardize ONE thing immediately
Modular product catalog with clear definitions — enable sales to build PS products and generate SOWs procedurally
Sales alignment and scoping: how Sales positions PS, customer requirements during pre-sales, tool-based migration scoping
Consistent discovery artifacts from every pre-sales engagement
Systematic, searchable retrospectives for continuous scoping improvement
Financial clarity: consistent billing approach across PS
Longer-term
Stronger EM and quicker path to production; unambiguous billable time scenarios; post-sales change management and risk management plans
Define customer segments from a PS perspective — run maturity assessments to inform change and risk management plans, consistently from pre-sales through post-sales
AI-enabled and/or automated pre-sales workflow steps; comprehensive PS Content Library
Detailed retrospectives for pre-sales deals and closed post-sales engagements

Methodology & Links06 / 06
Survey methodology,
notes & links
Question categories
Current offerings deficits and operational frictions — evaluating the execution gap: where do processes break down between sales, scoping, and post-sales delivery? Includes project failures, delivery blockers, and confidence ratings.
Definitions and terminology alignment — probing how different team members define foundational PS concepts. Highlighted semantic gaps that lead to scope creep or delivery friction.
Future offerings requirements — how to standardize offerings, what to consider, thought process behind it. Includes value definition, pre-sales and post-sales improvements.
Notes
Respondents were asked to specify their role at the start of the survey. Roles were used to correlate responses to role distribution where appropriate.
Sample size was ~30% of the PS team (April 2026). 50%+ of respondents were SAs (expected); the rest were EMs and PS Ops team members.
Many questions had multiple-option checkboxes — some percentage totals will exceed 100%.
This survey was designed to establish an internal baseline. A retest is recommended in FY28 to measure progress.
